The Finest Hours

One of the best movies ever made


The Story

This 2016 Disney film tells the harrowing true story of a daring coast guard rescue that happened off the coast of Massuchesetts in February 1952. This tale encapsulates the rescue of a T2 tanker that had split in half with 32 sailors still on board. Bernie Webber, a young member of the United States Coastguard, is tasked to attempt to rescue them. This is a story of love, courage, and teamwork that will have you on the edge of your seat.

    • Film Facts:
    1. This movie is based on the book by Casey Sherman and Michael J. Tougias.
    2. The movie was filmed in Chatham, Cohasset, Duxbury, Marshfield, Norwell, and Quincy in 2014.
    3. The film crew built an 800,000 gallon water tank to replicate the storm affects in.

    Historical Facts:
        The US Coast Guard Station in Chatham, Cape Cod sento out a for man rescue team in 60-foot waves to rescue them.
        There were two T2 tankers that had split the day of the incident, the Fort Mercer and the Pendleton.
        The events that took place that day are named the greatest small-boat rescue in all of Coast Guard history.
